About Me

My name is Iqra Javed. I focus on business intelligence, analytics, and designing systems that help organizations understand what is really happening inside their business.

I am not interested in dashboards that only look good. I care about correct numbers, clear logic, and systems that decision-makers can actually trust.

My work is about turning messy data into structured models, and turning those models into simple, reliable views of reality.

How I Think About Data

For me, analytics is not about charts. It is about building a reliable representation of the business.

Every number should:

  • Have a clear definition
  • Come from a controlled data model
  • Be consistent everywhere it appears
  • Support a real business decision

I prefer fewer metrics, but ones that are correct, stable, and meaningful.

How I Work

  1. Understand the business — What decisions are being made? What problems exist?
  2. Design the model — Tables, relationships, logic, definitions.
  3. Define the metrics — Clear, simple, controlled KPIs.
  4. Build the views — Only what is needed, nothing extra.
  5. Validate the numbers — Trust is more important than speed.

This approach avoids chaos, metric confusion, and “multiple versions of the truth”.

Long-Term Direction

I am building my skills and portfolio to work on serious data systems — revenue, finance, operations, and performance management.

My goal is to become someone who designs decision infrastructure, not just reports.

This website is part of that long-term strategy.
